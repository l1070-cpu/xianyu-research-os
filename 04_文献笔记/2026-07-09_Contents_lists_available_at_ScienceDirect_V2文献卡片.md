ABSTRACT
Hepatic nuclear factor 4 alpha (HNF4 α) drives the expression of apolipoprotein B (ApoB), microsomal trigly-
ceride transfer protein (MTP) and phospholipase A2 G12B (PLA2G12B), governing hepatic very-low-density
lipoprotein (VLDL) production and secretion. Andrographolide (AP) is a major constituent isolated from
Andrographis paniculata . We found that AP can disrupt the interaction between HNF4 α and its coactivator
peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor gamma coactivator 1-alpha (PGC1 α). Virtual docking and mutational
analysis indicated that arginine 235 of HNF4 α is essential for binding to AP. As a consequence of antagonizing
the activity of HNF4 α, AP suppresses the expression of ApoB, MTP and PLA2G12B and reduces the rate of
hepatic VLDL secretion in vivo . AP additionally reduced gluconeogenesis via down-regulating the expression of
HNF4α target genes phosphoenolpyruvate carboxykinase (Pepck) and glucose-6-phosphatase (G6pc).
Collectively, our results suggest that AP a ﬀects liver function via modulating the transcriptional activity of
HNF4α.
1. Introduction
Hepatic nuclear factor 4 alpha (HNF4 α) is a nuclear hormone re-
ceptor essential for liver development ( Hang et al., 2017; Lau et al.,
2018; Thakur et al., 2019 ). HNF4 α governs major hepatic functions
through directly binding to response elements located adjacent to its
target genes and guiding their expression ( Gonzalez, 2008; Moore et al.,
2016; Sheena et al., 2005 ). Both carbohydrate and lipid metabolisms
are subjected to ﬁne-tuning by HNF4 α (Galcheva et al., 2019; Hayhurst
et al., 2001; Yamagata, 2014). Speci ﬁcally, HNF4 α controls hepatic
very-low-density lipoprotein (VLDL) production and secretion through
target genes apolipoprotein B ( ApoB), microsomal triglyceride transfer
protein ( MTP) and phospholipase A2 G12B ( PLA2G12B)( Dai and
Hussain, 2012; Guan et al., 2011 ; Wang and Burke, 2008). Additionally,
HNF4α reﬁnes the rate of gluconeogenesis by dictating the expression
of phosphoenolpyruvate carboxykinase (Pepck) and glucose-6-
phosphatase (G6pc) ( Chavalit et al., 2013; Xu et al., 2007).
Known for its bitter taste, Andrographis paniculata is used tradi-
tionally as a medicinal herb in India to ﬁght infection and in China to
alleviate fever associated with infection ( Hossain et al., 2014 ; Hu et al.,
2017). Andrographolide (AP) is a major constituent isolated from An-
drographis paniculata with strong anti-microbial and anti-in ﬂammatory
activities ( Abu-Ghefreh et al., 2009 ; Arifullah et al., 2013 ). Recent
pharmacological studies in animal models suggest that AP can also
confer protective function on the liver upon dietary- or toxin-induced
stress ( Cabrera et al., 2017 ; Thakur et al., 2016). Of note, both carbo-
hydrate and lipid metabolisms are speci ﬁcally modulated by AP; how-
ever, the mechanistic basis behind these e ﬀects are still largely un-
known.

2. Materials and methods
2.1. Cloning and plasmids
The human HNF4 α-LBD fragment and human LXR α-LBD fragment
were subcloned into pCMV-BD vector to generate Gal4-HNF4 α LBD and
Gal4-LXRα LBD fusion constructs. pFR-Luciferase reporter plasmid was
purchased from Stratagene (Agilent Technologies, 211344). Human
PGC1α and mouse HNF4 α were cloned into pcDNA4 vector as pre-
viously reported ( Guan et al., 2011 ; Wang et al., 2009). The luciferase
reporter plasmids containing the mouse ApoB promoter ( −1981 to
+72), MTP promoter (−1823 to +135), PLA2G12B promoter (−1215
to +111) and G6pc promoter ( −934 to +22) were generated by PCR
and cloned into the Kpn Ⅰ (upstream) and XhoⅠ (downstream) sites of
pGL3 vector. The mouse Pepck promoter ( −1123 to +521) was sub-
cloned into pGL3 vector using Mlu Ⅰ and Xho Ⅰ restriction sites. Full
length mouse HNF4 α was subcloned into pcDNA3.1-HA vector. The
HNF4α-LBD mutant construct was generated by PCR-based mutagen-
esis using the QuikChange Site-Directed Mutagenesis Kit (Stratagene,
200518).

2.2. Protein-ligand docking analysis
The published crystal structure of HNF4 α (PDB: 4B7W) was used for
modelling of possible binding modes. All crystallographic water mole-
cules were deleted. The crystal structure was prepared for docking
using the protein preparation wizard in Maestro (Version 9.6,
Schrödinger), which assigns bond orders and adds hydrogens and
missing atoms. The active site was deﬁ ned with Receptor Grid
Generation model. Glide was used for the protein-ligand docking in XP
protocol. Multiple stereoisomers, ionization states, and 3D structures of
AP as input to the docking calculation were initially generated by
LigPrep using the OPLS_2005 force ﬁeld. Docked binding modes were
ranked using the Docking Score and manually inspected for the rea-
sonable interaction. Docking ﬁgure was generated using PyMOL.
